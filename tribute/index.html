Richard Stallman

Free Software Pioneer

Richard Stallman with a toy gnu, the symbol of the GNU project

Key events in Richard Stallman's life (as of 2016)

  • Born in 1953 in New York City
  • Enrolled at Harvard University in 1970 and became a programmer at the MIT Artificial Intelligence Laboratory. Graduated with a degree in Physics and started the graduate progamme in Physics at MIT which he ended after one year in favour of his work at the AI lab here he became a critic of restricions on computer access
  • Announced the GNU project in 1983 to create a free operating system and left MIT in 1984 to work full-time on the project
  • Wrote the GNU General Public License in 1989 to guarantee end users the freedoms to use, study, copy, modify, redistribute their software
  • Became an outspoken and widely travelled advocate of free software and opponent of copyright and surveillance

Software Projects initiated by Stallman

  • GNU Emacs, first public release in 1985
  • GNU Debugger, 1986
  • GNU Compiler Collection (initially, the GNU C Compiler), 1987
  • GNU Make (co-author with Roland McGrath), 1988

“Free software is a political movement; open source is a development model.”
